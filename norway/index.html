Norway
in Summer

Bygøy Peninsula
Museum Row
Monday · June 15
Day Trip
Norsk Folkemuseum · Bygøy
Morning
Norwegian Folk Museum
150+ historic buildings across open-air grounds. A 13th-century stave church, farmsteads and costumed interpreters in period character. Allow 2–3 hours.
Open-Air Museum
Afternoon
Kon-Tiki & Fram Museums
The actual balsa raft that crossed the Pacific. Then the Fram polar ship — the boys can walk the deck where Amundsen’s crew slept in tiny bunks.
Maritime History
Evening
Ferry Back & Pizza Night
Short fjord ferry back to Aker Brygge. Peppes Pizza for dinner. A Norwegian institution and a crowd-pleasing end to a full museum day.
Rest Night
Transport
⛴ Ruter Ferry Line 91
Aker Brygge → Bygøy · 10 min
Dining
Peppes Pizza — Classic Norwegian pizza chain. Kids love it without exception.
⭐ Kids Will Love This
Walking the deck of the Fram polar ship and peering into the crew bunks where real Arctic explorers slept.
💡
Practical Tip — Arrive at the Folk Museum at opening time. Costumed guides and farm animals draw tour groups fast. Head to the stave church and farmsteads first.

Day Three
Oslo Fjord Islands
Beach & Nature Day
Tuesday · June 16
Day Trip
Langøyene · Oslo Fjord
Morning
Langøyene Island Beach
Oslo’s best sandy beach island. Car-free, flat, warm fjord water for swimming. Wide grassy meadows behind the shore. Arrive early on sunny days.
Beach Day
Afternoon
Gressholmen Nature Walk
Wilder, forested island with bird-rich scrub, scramble rocks, ruins of Norway’s first commercial airport (1930s) and a seasonal café.
Nature Walk
Evening
Ferry Back & Harbor Pølse
Return ferry to Aker Brygge. Hot dogs (pølse) from a harbor kiosk — a genuine Norwegian tradition. Three beautifully exhausted boys.
Waterfront
Transport
⛴ Ruter Ferry Line 94
Aker Brygge → Islands · 15–25 min
Dining
Packed Picnic — Prepare the night before. Island food options are very limited and sell out fast.
⭐ Kids Will Love This
Riding the public ferry to a car-free island — the boys will feel like real Viking sea explorers on their first crossing.
💡
Practical Tip — Pack a complete picnic. Bring water shoes — the rocks around Gressholmen are slippery. A windbreak jacket is essential even on sunny June days on the fjord.

Thursday · June 18
City Day
Vigeland Sculpture Park · Oslo
Morning
Vigeland Sculpture Park
200+ sculptures across 80 free acres. The granite Monolith is the centrepiece. Children are actively encouraged to climb and touch the sculptures. Free entry, always open.
Free Entry
Afternoon
Frogner Park & Playground
The wider surrounding park has dedicated playgrounds, paddling pool areas and open lawns — where Oslo families come to relax on summer afternoons.
Local Life
Evening
Majorstuen & Golden Hour
Short metro hop for dinner in the Majorstuen café district. Consider a late 9–10 PM stroll back through Vigeland in the extraordinary midnight-sun golden light.
Golden Hour
Transport
🚶 T-bane + Walk
Lines 2/3 to Majorstuen, 5-min walk to park · 10 min
Dining
Local Café, Majorstuen — Many options. Ask for the “Dagens” lunch special — best value in any Norwegian café.
⭐ Kids Will Love This
Finding the angriest and most bizarre Vigeland sculptures and inventing stories about them — the Sinnataggen angry baby wins every time.
💡
Practical Tip — Vigeland at 9–10 PM in June is extraordinary — amber light on granite. Worth a short after-dinner stroll even with tired small boys. Bring a light jacket.

Day Six
Oslo → Bergen
The Bergen Railway
Friday · June 19
Overnight BergenBook Early
Bergen Railway (Bergensbanen) · Hardangervidda
Morning
Board Bergen Railway at Oslo S
One of the world’s great scenic train journeys. Book an early departure. Pack a full activity bag per child — snacks, headphones, drawing notebooks — for the crossing.
Scenic Train
Afternoon
Cross Hardangervidda Plateau
Finse station at 1,222m — snow on the peaks in June. Descend through dramatic fjord-carved valleys toward Bergen as the daylight stretches toward evening.
Mountain Crossing
Evening
Arrive Bergen — Bryggen First Look
Check in, short walk along the colourful Bryggen wharf waterfront. Bergen Fish Market stalls for dinner — fish soup, fish cakes and fresh shrimp on the harbour.
Arrival Night
Transport
🚂 Vy Bergen Railway
Oslo S → Bergen direct · ~7 hrs
Dining
Bergen Fish Market (Fisketorget) — Hot food stalls on the harbour. Fish soup, fish cakes and shrimp — excellent for kids.
⭐ Kids Will Love This
Seeing snow-covered mountain peaks from the train window in the middle of summer — the boys will flat-out refuse to believe it.
💡
Practical Tip — Build a Norway Train Kit for each child: drawing notebook, sticker book, snacks, headphones with downloaded content for the mountain tunnels. The onboard café car has warm food and drinks.
🔔 Advance Booking Required — Bergen Railway sells out weeks ahead in June. Book 6–8 weeks early via vy.no. Request right-side seats facing Bergen for the best views across Hardangervidda.

Day Seven
Bergen
Bryggen, Fløyen & Fish Market
Saturday · June 20
Overnight Bergen
Bryggen Wharf · Bergen (UNESCO)
Morning
Bryggen Wharf (UNESCO)
14th-century Hanseatic timber buildings on Bergen harbour. Explore the narrow “passasjer” alleyways, hidden courtyards and craftsman shops. A UNESCO World Heritage Site.
UNESCO Heritage
Afternoon
Fløibanen Funicular to Fløyen
8-minute ride to the 320m summit. Panoramic fjord views, playground and café. The Trollskogen Troll Forest trail has carved wooden troll statues hidden among the trees.
Mountain Views
Evening
Bergen Fish Market & Waterfront
Fresh shrimp, king crab and fish cakes at Fisketorget. Optional: Bergen Aquarium with penguins and Atlantic seals is a short walk from the harbour.
Local Market
Transport
🚶 Walking Only
All Bergen sights within 10-min walk · 5–10 min
Dining
Pingvinen, Bergen — Traditional Norwegian home cooking in a casual, family-welcoming setting.
⭐ Kids Will Love This
Finding the carved wooden troll statues hidden throughout the Trollskogen forest at the top of Mount Fløyen.
💡
Practical Tip — Bergen rains approximately 250 days a year. Pack full waterproofs for every family member, every single day. Weather shifts from sunshine to downpour within minutes.

Flåm Railway & Nærøyfjord
The Longest Day
Sunday · June 21
Summer SolsticeCritical BookingOvernight Flåm
Flåm Railway (Flamsbana) · Nærøydal
Morning
Bergen → Myrdal Mountain Train
Vy train from Bergen to Myrdal (2 hrs). Dramatic valley and mountain scenery. Transfer at the remote Myrdal junction station for the Flåm Railway descent.
Scenic Train

865m descent through 20km of vertical Norwegian mountain. The Kjosfossen waterfall stop — the Huldra dances in the mist. Then: UNESCO Nærøyfjord fjord cruise from Flåm village.
Bucket List
Evening
Solstice Night in Flåm
Dinner at Ægir Bryggeri — a Viking-themed brewpub with extraordinary timber interiors. The sun will not fully set tonight. Let the boys stay up just a little longer.
Solstice Night
“

The sun will not fully set tonight. The Kjosfossen waterfall, the Huldra in the mist, the Nærøyfjord at midnight light — this is the day the whole trip is built around.

Transport
🚂 Vy + Flåm Railway
Bergen → Myrdal → Flåm (Flamsbana) · ~4 hrs to Flåm
Dining
Ægir Bryggeri, Flåm — Viking-themed brewpub. Extraordinary atmosphere. Book a table in advance for summer.
⭐ Kids Will Love This
The Kjosfossen waterfall stop — the train pauses, everyone steps onto the platform, and the Huldra forest spirit appears dancing in the mist above the falls.

🔔 CRITICAL Advance Booking — Flåm Railway sells out many weeks ahead in June. Book as early as possible — ideally 6–8 weeks. Also book the Nærøyfjord boat cruise and Bergen → Myrdal train separately in advance.

Day Nine

Scenic Return Journey
Monday · June 22
Confirm Connection
Nærøyfjord · UNESCO World Heritage
Morning
Final Walk in Flåm Village
Gentle fjord-shore walk to the 1670 Flåm Church (Flåmskyrkja) — one of Norway’s oldest wooden churches. Boys skip stones in the still Aurlandsfjord water.
Village Walk
Afternoon
Flåm Railway Up → Oslo Train
Ride the Flamsbana back uphill — same dramatic scenery in reverse. Connect at Myrdal to the Oslo-bound Bergen Railway. Long but beautiful journey home.
Return Train
Evening
Arrive Back at Oslo Home Base
Quiet apartment dinner, laundry, repack. Early night. The fjords, the Huldra, the midnight sun — time to process it all.
Home Base
Transport
🚂 Flamsbana + Vy
Flåm → Myrdal → Oslo S · ~5.5–6 hrs total
Dining
Home-Cooked Apartment Dinner — Grocery stop after arriving in Oslo. Simple meal, early night for everyone.
⭐ Kids Will Love This
The Flåm Railway going uphill — the same waterfalls and cliffs in reverse, and they’ll spot new details they missed on the way down.
💡
Practical Tip — Buy the return Flåm Railway tickets the evening before at Flåm station. Build at least 35 minutes of buffer at Myrdal. Missing the Oslo connection means a very cold wait at an exposed mountain station.
🔔 Confirm Connection — Check your Myrdal → Oslo train time the day before at Flåm station. Only a few departures daily from Myrdal — missing it is a significant problem with young children.

Day Eleven
Drøbak
Santa’s Village & Oslo Fjord Town
Wednesday · June 24
Day Trip
Drøbak · Oslo Fjord
Morning
Drøbak Old Town & Julenissen’s Post Office
Preserved coastal wooden-house town on the fjord. The Jul i Drøbak Christmas shop (open year-round) is the official Norwegian address of Father Christmas.
Christmas Town
Afternoon
Oscarsborg Fortress Ferry
Short ferry to the fortress island that sank the German cruiser Blücher on April 9, 1940 — giving the royal family the hours they needed to escape Oslo. Plus Drøbak Akvarium.
History & Nature
Evening
Return to Oslo & Final Packing
Bus back to Oslo. Last dinner out or in. Begin sorting and packing for tomorrow’s departure. Last of the Freia chocolate consumed.
Final Night
Transport
🚌 Ruter Bus 541
Rådhusplassen → Drøbak (return) · 50–60 min each way
Dining
Havnestua, Drøbak Harbour — Simple Norwegian fish dishes on the water with direct fjord views.
⭐ Kids Will Love This
Mailing a postcard from Julenissen’s official Norwegian post office — addressed to themselves, arriving home after they do. Magic for the youngest two.
💡
Practical Tip — Buy a postcard and stamp at Jul i Drøbak and post it from the local box. The card arriving home weeks after the family is one of those small rituals children remember for years.

Departure Day
Oslo Opera House · Bjørvika
Morning
Oslo Opera House Rooftop

Opera/MUNCH walkable from Oslo S · Flytoget to OSL · 20 min to airport
Dining
Kaffistova, Oslo — Classic Norwegian home cooking. Kjøttkaker and fish cakes. Excellent value for a family farewell dinner.

Racing up the sloping white marble roof of the Oslo Opera House from the water’s edge — whoever reaches the top first wins Norway.

Practical Tip — Allow 2.5–3 hours from accommodation to gate at Gardermoen. Flytoget from Oslo S departs every 10–20 minutes. Do not cut this close with three children and luggage.
