Section 02 Practical & Traveler Tips
🚌
Getting Around
  • Ruter 7-day pass: Buy before you land. Covers all Oslo buses, trams, metro and fjord island ferries in Zone 1. Kids under 6 ride free. On weekends one adult can bring up to 4 children under 18 for free.
  • Strollers on Ruter: Fold strollers on buses during rush hour. Oslo trams have low floors and dedicated stroller areas. Ferries have large flat decks — strollers are easy.
  • Vy app for trains: Download before departure. Book family carriages (Familievogn) on Lillehammer and Bergen routes — they include dedicated play areas for the 4 and 6-year-olds.
  • Flytoget (airport express): 20 minutes from Oslo Gardermoen (OSL) to Oslo S. Buy tickets on the train or at airport machines. The standard Vy regional train also runs the same route for less.
💳
Money & Budgeting
  • Norway is cashless: Virtually every business accepts contactless Visa/Mastercard. Do not bother exchanging cash — you will almost never need it.
  • Grocery strategy: Kiwi, Rema 1000 and Meny are the main supermarket chains. Buying breakfast and packed lunches here cuts daily food costs by 60–70%.
  • Tipping: Not required or expected. Rounding up to the nearest whole number at a sit-down dinner is appreciated but entirely optional.
  • Museum costs: Vigeland Park, Ekebergparken and Opera House rooftop are all free. Budget NOK 200–350 per adult for the Fram, Kon-Tiki and Maihaugen.
  • Children’s discounts: Most Norwegian museums offer half-price or free entry for children under 16. Always ask at the desk.
🌧
Weather & Packing
  • June range: Oslo averages 18–22°C (64–72°F) in June but can swing to 10°C (50°F) with wind and rain on the same afternoon. Bergen is wetter — expect rain at least one of your two nights.
  • Layer system: Base layer (merino wool), mid-layer (light fleece), waterproof outer shell. All five family members. Every single day.
  • Rain can be sudden: Pack waterproof jackets in your day bag regardless of the morning forecast. The fjords create their own micro-weather systems.
  • Sun protection: June daylight is 18+ hours in Oslo. Apply SPF on exposed skin for all outdoor days — the sun stays high and intense even in the evening.
🏥
Safety & Health
  • Emergency numbers: Medical emergency: 113. Police: 112. Fire: 110. Non-urgent medical help: 116 117.
  • Oslo urgent care: Oslo kommunale legevakt at Storgata 40 — the main 24-hour emergency clinic. English is widely spoken at all Norwegian healthcare facilities.
  • Travel insurance: Ensure your family policy covers emergency medical evacuation. Norwegian private care is expensive without EHIC or travel insurance.
  • Tap water: Completely safe, cold and delicious across Norway. Bring five reusable bottles — refill everywhere and save significant money.
  • Key Norwegian phrases: “Hjelp!” (Help!). “Ring en lege” (Call a doctor). “Barnet mitt er syk” (My child is sick). Locals will immediately respond in fluent English.
🤝
Cultural Etiquette
  • Personal space: Norwegians value quiet on public transport. Keep children’s noise at a reasonable level on trains and ferries — especially on long journeys.
  • Allemannsretten: Norway’s “Right to Roam” means you can walk freely in any natural landscape. Teach the 10-year-old: leave no trace, take nothing, disturb nothing.
  • “Takk” and “Hei”: Teaching all three boys these two words will generate genuine warmth from every Norwegian they encounter.
  • Queue culture: Norwegians queue properly and patiently. Model the same behaviour for the children at ferry and train terminals.
🎪
Keeping Kids Engaged
  • Train activity kits: For the Bergen Railway and Lillehammer trips, pack new activities revealed only on the train: sticker books, magnetic drawing boards, audiobooks on headphones.
  • Junior explorer journal: Give the 10-year-old a notebook to sketch and journal. The 6-year-old can draw what he sees. The 4-year-old gets stickers. All three stay occupied.
  • Snack strategy: Norwegian “matpakke” (packed sandwiches in paper) is the local tradition. Make sandwich-packing a morning activity the boys participate in.
  • The 4-year-old’s limits: Build a genuine nap or quiet rest into every third afternoon. A tired 4-year-old can derail an entire day. Schedule before need.
  • Playground hunting: Oslo has extraordinary neighbourhood playgrounds. Let the 10-year-old navigate to the nearest one on Google Maps — it gives him purpose and buys everyone 45 minutes.
☀
Midnight Sun & Sleep Management
  • Oslo sunset in June: Astronomical twilight never ends. The sky remains a pale blue-grey at its darkest around 1 AM. Without blackout, children will not sleep.
  • Blackout window solution: Portable travel blackout blinds with suction cups are the single most important purchase for this trip. Buy before you leave.
  • Tin foil backup: If the blinds fail, tape tin foil across the children’s bedroom window. It works. Use gaffer tape, not masking tape.
  • Maintain bedtime routine: The sun lying about the time of day is disorienting for young children. A consistent bedtime routine (bath, story, lights out) anchors the schedule regardless of what the sky looks like.
  • Solstice night (June 21): The sun barely dips below the horizon at Bergen. Plan for this in advance. Do not assume the children will be tired because you are.
  • Melatonin: Consult your paediatrician before the trip about low-dose melatonin for the younger two on the first few nights. It can genuinely help reset the circadian rhythm.
Section 03 Public Transportation Guide
📱
Essential Apps
  • Ruter app: For all Oslo public transit — bus, tram, T-bane metro and fjord island ferries. Buy multi-day passes, plan routes, track real-time arrivals. Available iOS and Android. Official site: ruter.no/en
  • Vy app: For intercity trains — Lillehammer, Bergen Railway, Myrdal connection. Book Minipris fares and select seats. Official site: vy.no/en
  • EnTur: Norway’s national journey planner covering all transport modes nationwide including rural buses and ferry connections. Search: “entur.no journey planner Norway”
🚂
Trains with Young Kids
  • Family carriages: Vy regional trains include Familievogn (family carriages) with play areas. Book these specifically when reserving — they are not automatically assigned.
  • Strollers on trains: Fold and store in the dedicated luggage vestibule at the end of each carriage. Never leave in the aisle.
  • Onboard food: The Bergen Railway has a café car serving hot food, sandwiches and drinks. Prices are high — supplement with your own snacks.
  • Window sides: For Bergen Railway, request right-side seats (facing Bergen) for the best mountain and fjord views across Hardangervidda.
⛴
Ferries & Island Boats
  • Oslo fjord ferries: Lines 91 (Aker Brygge → Bygøy) and 94 (Aker Brygge → Langøyene) are standard Ruter public transport — included in your 7-day pass.
  • Nærøyfjord cruise: Book separately through Fjord Tours or the Flåm Railway combo. Advance booking essential for June. Search: “Nærøyfjord cruise booking 2026”
  • Boarding with kids: Ferries have open flat decks. Keep the 4-year-old by the hand when boarding and alighting. Life jackets are available onboard for children.

Section 05 Family Packing List

Tailored for outdoor adventure travel in Norway in June with children ages 4, 6 and 10. Click any item to mark it packed.

Clothing & Layers (All 5 Family Members)
  • Lightweight waterproof rain jacket — pack in day bag every day
  • Mid-weight fleece or wool pullover
  • Merino wool long-sleeve base layer (2 per person)
  • Quick-dry walking trousers or leggings (avoid heavy denim)
  • Swimsuits and quick-dry towels (fjord island beach days)
  • Light hat or cap (sun protection on long daylight days)
  • Comfortable sturdy walking shoes (no heavy hiking boots needed)
  • Warm socks (merino wool if available)
Rain & Wind Protection
  • Waterproof rain trousers for each child (Bergen and fjord days)
  • Waterproof rain boots for the 4-year-old (small puddles are irresistible)
  • Compact travel umbrella (adults)
  • Windproof fleece-lined neck gaiter (fjord boat days)
  • Waterproof dry bag for ferry and island days
Sun Protection (18+ Hours of Daylight)
  • SPF 50 sunscreen — one bottle per child, one per adult
  • UV-protection sunglasses for kids and adults
  • Wide-brim sun hat for the 4-year-old
  • Travel blackout blinds with suction cups (CRITICAL for kids’ sleep)
  • Sleep masks for parents
  • Gaffer tape (backup for blocking light around window frames)
Child Comfort Items for Long Travel Days
  • Headphones for each child (noise-cancelling ideal for trains)
  • Tablets/devices loaded with downloaded shows, games and audiobooks
  • New sticker books — revealed only on long train journeys
  • Small magnetic drawing board for the 4-year-old
